SECOND: Mitigating Perceptual Hallucination in Vision-Language Models via Selective and Contrastive Decoding

ICML 2025
Woohyeon Park1, Woojin Kim1, Jaeik Kim1, Jaeyoung Do1
1Seoul National University
Code

Abstract

We propose SECOND: Selective and Contrastive Decoding, a novel approach that enables VLMs to effectively leverage multi-scale visual information with an object-centric manner, closely aligning with human visual perception.

Overview of SECOND

Starting with sparse visual information in Stage 1, SECOND incorporates higher scales' fine-grained information selectively based on visual attention. By contrasting each interval of stages, it achieves the final output effectively mitigating perceptual hallucination.

SECOND Visualization

Evolutionary attention refinement in SECOND. Red areas represent high attention scores. SECOND gradually enhances visual attention, demonstrating robustness across diverse object scales.
